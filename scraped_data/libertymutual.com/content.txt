What is a small commercial auto insurance policy?
Commercial auto insurance helps pay for bodily injuries and property damage sustained by others when you or an employee causes an accident while driving a company-owned, personal, or leased vehicle for small business purposes. You can typically add collision and comprehensive coverage to a policy to cover the cost of repairs to the business's vehicles as well.
What's covered under a small commercial auto insurance policy
Commercial auto insurance generally includes coverage for:
Liability costs for third-party bodily injuries and property damage stemming from an accident you or one of your employees cause while driving a company-owned, personal, or leased vehicle
Medical expenses for you, your employees, and passengers when injured in a motor vehicle accident
Physical damage to your vehicle from an accident (collision coverage) or something else (comprehensive coverage)
Bodily injuries and property damage caused by underinsured or uninsured motorists

Who needs small commercial auto insurance?
Any small business that uses one or more company-owned, personal, or leased vehicles for business purposes needs commercial auto insurance.
An owner's or employee's personal auto insurance policy will not cover vehicles driven for business purposes.
Small commercial auto insurance provides valuable protection for businesses that
Utilize cars, trucks, or vans to conduct business (bakeries, hardware stores, etc.)
Attend meetings or visit jobsites (lawyers, interior decorators, architects, engineers, etc.)
Haul equipment or tools to jobsites (carpenters, electricians, etc.)
Use light trucks and construction vehicles (builders, general contractors, etc.)
